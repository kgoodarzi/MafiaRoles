What is Mafia?

Mafia is a social deduction game that simulates a conflict between an informed minority (the mafia) and an uninformed majority (the citizens). The game was created by Dimitry Davidoff in 1986 and has become popular worldwide under various names like "Werewolf" and "Assassin."

Basic Rules

  1. Players are secretly assigned roles as either Mafia members or Citizens.
  2. The game alternates between "night" and "day" phases.
  3. During the night, the Mafia secretly choose a Citizen to eliminate.
  4. Special roles may perform their actions during the night.
  5. During the day, all players discuss and vote to eliminate one player they suspect is Mafia.
  6. Citizens win if they eliminate all Mafia members.
  7. Mafia wins if they equal or outnumber the Citizens.

Tips for Players

  • Pay attention to everyone's behavior and statements.
  • As a Citizen, share as much information as possible to help identify the Mafia.
  • As Mafia, try to blend in and avoid drawing suspicion.
  • Special roles should be careful about revealing their identity too early.
